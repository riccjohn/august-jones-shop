## The Core Problem: Likes ≠ Sales

If your Instagram ads are set to an **Engagement objective**, Meta optimizes delivery to find people most likely to tap the heart button — not buy a hoodie. These are different people.

| Objective | Meta Optimizes For | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement | Likes, comments, shares | Only for amplifying social proof |
| Traffic | Link clicks to store | **Right now** — to build pixel/retargeting data |
| Sales/Conversions | Purchases | Once you have 50+ purchase events in the pixel |

**Immediate action:** Check your campaign objective in Meta Ads Manager. If it's Engagement, switch to Traffic.

## Converting Instagram to Sales

### The Funnel Gap
Social media traffic converts at **0.5–2%** — far below email (3–5%) or organic search (2–4%). Every extra click loses potential buyers.

Current path: Instagram bio → marketing website → Etsy = **3 clicks before purchase intent registers**. The marketing site is a detour.

**Fix:** Point your Instagram bio directly to your Etsy shop.

### Content by Format

| Format | Role | What to Post |
|---|---|---|
| **Reels** | Discovery (top-funnel) | Jersey → hoodie transformation, before/after, "one-of-one" reveals |
| **Feed/Carousel** | Trust building | Materials, jersey backstory, craft process, outfit styling |
| **Stories** | Conversion (bottom-funnel) | "This piece ships Monday," countdown, direct product link, DM prompts |

- Reels get **2.25x higher reach** than feed posts
- Reels are how cold audiences discover you; Stories close warm followers

### Instagram Shopping (Product Tags)
Tag products directly in posts → skips the bio link step entirely → estimated **15–25% conversion uplift**.

Setup (Etsy): Facebook Commerce Manager → catalog sync → tag pieces in posts.

For one-of-a-kind pieces: create Etsy listing → tag in Instagram post → archive when sold.

### DMs as a Sales Channel
**150 million people DM businesses on Instagram monthly.** Treat every "Is this still available?" as a hot lead — respond fast, close the sale.

- **ManyChat automation:** Comment trigger (e.g., comment "JERSEY") → auto-DM with product link
- D2C case study: ManyChat automation → 20% more orders + 150K new followers in 3 months
- **Instagram Broadcast Channel:** 90%+ open-rate drop announcements — better than email for Instagram-native audiences

### One-of-a-Kind as a Conversion Superpower
The upcycled fashion market: $7.4B (2024) → projected $11.66B by 2029. Mass brands can't replicate this.

**Effective framing:** "This started as a 2003 Packers jersey. There's one. Here it is." — specific, authentic, time-pressured. Every post is a limited-edition drop announcement.

Post sold-out content: it builds FOMO and proves demand.

## Meta/Instagram Paid Ads

### ROAS Benchmarks
| Level | ROAS |
|---|---|
| Fashion median (Meta) | 2.65x |
| Target for August Jones | **2.5x minimum** |
| Strong performance | 3.0–4.5x |
| Retargeting campaigns | avg 4.2x |
| Below 2x sustained | needs fixing |

### Best Ad Formats
1. **Reels ads** — 22–35% higher CTR than feed, 40% lower CPC for retail → best for top-of-funnel discovery
2. **Stories ads** — 14% cheaper, 78% more clicks → best for retargeting/conversion
3. **Feed/Carousel** — middle-funnel product storytelling

### 3-Stage Funnel on a Small Budget ($10–20/day)

**Stage 1 — Build audience (Weeks 1–4, $5–10/day)**
- Objective: Video Views or Awareness
- Creative: Reels — transformation process, jersey backstory, founder story
- Audience: Broad interest targeting
- Do NOT run conversion campaigns yet — no pixel data to optimize from

**Stage 2 — Drive traffic (Weeks 3–6, $5–7/day)**
- Objective: Traffic
- Creative: Carousel of pieces, product spotlights
- Audience: People who watched 50%+ of your Reels

**Stage 3 — Convert warm audiences (Week 5+, $5–10/day)**
- Objective: Sales (requires pixel)
- Creative: Single product, clear CTA, urgency, Stories format
- Audience: Website visitors last 30 days, cart abandoners
- Don't start until retargeting audience hits **1,000 people**

**Ongoing split:** 60% awareness/consideration, 40% conversion/retargeting

### Interest Targeting for August Jones
Layer with AND logic (narrow audience):
- Sports/athletics (NFL, NBA, college sports)
- Streetwear brands (Supreme, Champion, Jordan)
- Vintage/thrift/upcycled fashion
- Sustainable fashion
- Wisconsin-specific teams (Packers, Badgers, Brewers)

### Meta Pixel on Etsy
Install via: `Etsy Seller Hub → Marketing → Ads → Track conversions with Meta Pixel`

**Important limitation:** Etsy's pixel only fires on the checkout confirmation page. It cannot track product views or add-to-carts. Retargeting audiences will only include purchasers — a small pool.

This is why **Shopify migration is the #1 growth unlock** (see below).

### Warning Signs Your Ads Are Failing
- CTR below 1% → creative isn't resonating
- Frequency above 3–4 with flat ROAS → ad fatigue, refresh creative
- High link clicks but zero add-to-carts → store page problem, not ad problem
- ROAS below 1x for 7+ days → pause and reassess
- Engagement campaign with no store traffic increase → wrong objective

### Scale Signals (all 3 must be true)
1. ROAS at 3x+ consistently for 5–7 days
2. 50+ purchase events in the pixel in the last 7 days
3. Ad frequency below 2.5

**Scale method:** Increase budget 20% every 3–5 days max. Never double overnight — resets Meta's learning phase.

## KPIs to Track

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| AOV (Average Order Value) | Track & grow monthly |
| CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) | Under $40–50; must be < LTV ÷ 3 |
| LTV (Lifetime Value) | Must be 3x+ CAC |
| Repeat Purchase Rate | 25% = good start; 40%+ = strong |
| Gross Margin | 65%+ for handmade; 85%+ is excellent |
| ROAS | 2.5x minimum; 3x+ target |
| Etsy/Shopify conversion rate | 1.5%+ = healthy; 3%+ = strong |

## Path to Full-Time Revenue

### Milestones
- **Proof of concept:** 10+ sales to strangers at full price = model works
- **Side hustle viability:** $1,500–3,000/month consistently
- **Full-time threshold:** $4,000–5,000/month net (for Madison, WI cost of living)
- **Scale signal:** More demand than production capacity → raise prices, hire help, or develop drop model

### Handling the One-of-a-Kind Scaling Constraint
1. **Raise prices** — scarcity + handmade = premium pricing power
2. **Waitlist/pre-order** — customers commit before the piece is made
3. **Small-batch drops** — 3–5 jerseys from the same team/era as a coherent drop event
4. **Custom commissions** — customer sends their jersey, you transform it → highest margin
5. **Secondary products** — jersey scraps → hats, bags, accessories (see Psychic Outlaw model)

### Comparable Brands
- **Zero Waste Daniel** (NYC) — pre-consumer textile waste, each piece unique, pivoted to e-commerce
- **Psychic Outlaw** (Austin) — vintage quilts/bandanas, uses every scrap for secondary products
- **Assemblage Studio** (Michigan, 2021) — solo operation, military uniforms + vintage denim
- **Brackish** (Charleston) — turkey feather bow ties as a wedding gift → full business with team

### Build Your Owned Audience (Email List)
Instagram followers are rented. Algorithm changes or account issues = lost audience overnight. Email is owned.

- **Kit (ConvertKit)** — free up to 10K subscribers, creator-focused ([kit.com](https://kit.com))
- Offer: "Join the drop list — first access before Instagram sees it"
- Collect via: checkout, DM automation, website signup (in progress on marketing site)

## Strategic Milestone: Move to Shopify

Etsy is a great start but its ad tracking limitations cap paid ad effectiveness. Shopify enables:
- Full Meta Pixel + Conversions API (tracks every step of the funnel)
- Proper retargeting audiences (product views, add-to-carts, cart abandoners)
- Linkpop (free link-in-bio with catalog sync, by Shopify)
- Cart abandonment emails
- Full analytics on traffic sources

Priority: migrate to Shopify once sales are consistent.
